Onomatopoeia

 

I’ve set out to write a poem all in onomatopoeia

And I shall succeed

 

For, I know this is a stretch, but hear me out

Words are just the sounds humans can spout

 

If meow and moo and woof are onomatopoeic

Then our sounds are too

 

What are words but our sounds with meaning in tow?

All words are onomatopoeic – ciao, 你好, hello

 

I already hear your argument – those words have meaning

Ideas beyond the sound

 

So, what of words which have acquired additional uses?

Snap and boom and drip – so, what of these abuses? 

 

Snap is something breaking or the 4th derivative of position

Boom can sound the thunder or refer to an audio device

Drip might be a watery hit or that water falling off some ice 

 

My last argument is something we all know – KTV

A song sung in another language

 

If a singer knows not the language – just imitates the sound

Onomatopoeia is all that they sing – a musical rebound

 

I already hear your argument – then it depends upon the use

Well then friends, I’ve won

 

Simply remove the meaning from all the words I’ve needed

My arguments intact, lodged in your mind, but I have succeeded
