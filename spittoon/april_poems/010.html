Mind

 

I don’t mind – but I’m mindful

Hiding insight behind irreverence

Hiding scars behind sardonic quips

So, barring unkindness – I don’t mind

 

I’m not kind – but I purport kindness

Hiding anger behind veiled niceties

Hiding insecurity behind a helping hand

Without someone’s need – I’m not kind

 

I’m not needy – but I purport needlessness

Hiding desire behind a calm complacency

Hiding want behind a wisened detachment

So, barring true pain – I’m not needy

 

I’m not in pain – but I wear my scars

Wearing mistakes on the back of m’hands

Conversing with those who need an ear

Always ready to drop it all – I’m not in pain – I’m not needy – trying to be kind – and trying not to mind

 

I don’t mind – a fault of mine – maybe too accepting

Try to be kind – a lie I sell the world – sometimes I judge

I’m not needy – simple niceties – a spontaneous path

I’m not in pain – that’s others – I hurt for others only – a lie I sell myself

 

It’s difficult to think without respite – without an off button – but not to mind

It’s difficult to care always in a world which doesn’t care for your tears – so I hide

It’s difficult to want not in a world consumed by image and things – but I try to let go

It’s difficult to recognize the pain in others having felt the same – be kind but guard myself

 

So how do I feel and mind and think and desire in a world which protects itself?

Do I engage?  Do I feel?  Do I hurt?  Do I desire?  Do I mind?    

 

I don’t know... but that can be okay… that not knowing… I don’t mind

I don’t know… without convictions… without belief… can I be kind?

I don’t know… I have so much but not that much… I could let it go

I don’t know… do I bare m’soul to others… admit my pain is mine

I don’t know... but that can be okay… that not knowing… I don’t mind
