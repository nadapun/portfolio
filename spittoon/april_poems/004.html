Childlike Innocence

 

I lost my childlike innocence falling down the stairs after too many drinks

I lost my childlike innocence waking up in the hospital after a stupid choice

I lost my childlike innocence spurning a friend who I thought wronged me

I lost my childlike innocence failing and failing and failing at something that should be easy

I’ve lost my childlike innocence countless times ‘cause I’m forever a child

No consistency – no responsibility – a child

 

I lost my childlike innocence somewhere on the ice-covered bus stop

Being kicked and stomped and spat upon – lost my favorite hat too

I lost my childlike innocence running through traffic to protect a stranger

Stopping cars and sliding beside a motionless body – still breathing

I lost my childlike innocence saving her life, hundreds of miles away

And she calls me

I lost my childlike innocence keeping her alive – a different soul, the same story

She calls me

I’ve lost my childlike innocence countless times ‘cause I’m forever a child

No innocence but still a child

 

I’ll stand up to bullies on a winter bus knowing what’s coming my way

I’ll run in front of another bus to catch the stranger skidding by

I’ll answer her call or yours – I’ll listen – I’ll tell you why you should still be here

 

I don’t purport any wisdom or bravery, but the mistakes and pain I’ve endured

Put me in the right place to save some lives or at least stop some suffering

 

I’m not much of an adult, and I sure ain’t innocent

Though I can be contented just being a child if this is where it places me
