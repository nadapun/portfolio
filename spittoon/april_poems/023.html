Galleries

 

Lucky enough to have been to galleries worldwide

Much of the art must be observed up close

However, this poem is about benches

 

In galleries most people use the benches as respite

A tired mind, tired feet, a bored companion

Missing the true power of the bench

 

Sure, in galleries such as Rothko or Pollock – monsters

The bench is a source of competition

Everyone wants a seat absorbing giants

 

But what of benches too far from the detail expected

These are my favorite benches – cut off

It is not here I rest and clear my mind...

 

I observe – space and form, movement and calm, energy

The passage of people, some too concerned

Seeing my reverence – ask if they paid theirs?

 

I love museum benches for the mundane respect they pay

But I love wild benches all the more – rabid

Sitting alone pondering on a museum bench is one thing

 

The world is a gallery littered with places to observe

And so, I shall, sketchbook at hand and a pen

I draw statues and ideas and people and skies

 

So, this is an ode to benches, for, without them, I ask

Would we have galleries and art - reflection?

Would we have time for respite and pause?

 

In my grandiose ideations, my convoluted machinations

Benches give me calm and public invisibility

An artist sitting and thinking without responsibility

 

Praise to the mighty bench – champion of respite and rest

But so much more – forum, sanctuary, sight

Build benches, build monuments of pause

 

A bench – the hill I’ll die on? – my architectural cause?
