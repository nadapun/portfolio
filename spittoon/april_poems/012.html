Gnawing

 

This knowing sensation – or is it a gnawing sensation

A familiarity, yet

Something’s not quite right, but I look around

The rest of the crowd nodding in time – and still this gnawing

 

I’ve been here before – or I’ve dreamt this before

A recognition, yet

Something’s not quite right, collapsing in to the past

that time I don’ t speak of – the abstraction on my forearm

 

Spirals don’t repeat - a quick glance inward – a wave of defeat

I haven’t been here, yet

Something’s recognizable, the patterns the place, the face   

A gnawing sensation and this spiral quickens my heartbeat

 

I’m dancing back into the past – a strange shadow upon me cast

I’ve been here... I see it... I know you... let me explain

I’m spiraling, I’ve been here... I know where this goes... but not quite

Met with disbelief and concern, I know your face - splintered but I could never forget it or I just did or I’m spiraling again... your face and the spiral is all that will last.

I need to leave... the gnawing is back... spiral outwards – cleave

This has always been, yet

It’s time to go, stepping in to my next life – my next version

I wish I could stay but I have a new strand to explore, too many leaves

 

And so I walk away from your exploding face, take a breath, slow my pace

And the world is new again

A nubile branch of time unexplored and I forget the spiral, the collapse

Walking aimlessly down the thread, forgetting until that gnawing is retraced
